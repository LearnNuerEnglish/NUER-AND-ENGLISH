Thok Naath Time Phrases

Essential time-related expressions in Nuer (Thok Naath) and English.

Nuer (Thok Naath) English Translation
Jɛn ɛ thaak di̱?What time is it?
Ɛ thaak di̱?What’s the time?
Ɛ thaak wäl.It’s ten o’clock.
Ɛ wäär där.It’s midnight.
Ɛ thaak bäkɛl ɛn tämɛ.It’s 6 A.M. now.
Ban jëk kɛ thaak bädäk.See you at 8 P.M.
Thaak dä cɛ cuɔ̱ŋ.My watch is stopped.
Thaak dä ɛ niy-niy.My watch is slow.
Ca thaak jiɛɛnä.It’s time to leave.
Te kɔn kɛ thaak mi̱ ro̱ŋ.We have plenty of time.
Gua̱a̱th cɛ we̱ ɛn tämɛ.It’s too late now.

Daily Practice: Where? (Ni̱kä?)

Common questions and answers for locations and daily activities.

Nuer (Thok Naath) English Translation
Ji̱n ɛ ni̱kä ɛn tämɛ?Where are you now?
Ɣän nɛni̱.I am here.
Ji̱n ciëŋi̱ ni̱kä?Where do you live?
Ɣän Ciëŋä thiẹkä.I live nearby.
Wä ni ji̱n ni̱kä?Where are you going?
Ɣän wa̱a̱ ciëŋ.I am going home.
Ji̱n lät di̱ ni̱kä?Where do you work?
Ɣän La̱tä wa̱nɛ.I work here.
Jaa̱th du ja ni̱kä?Where is your phone?
Jɛn ja wi̱i̱ tharbëtä.It is on the table.
Duel puɔɔkä ja ni̱kä?Where is the bathroom?
Jɛn nii.It is there.
Ji̱n gɔri ni̱kä?Where do you study?
Ɣän gɔ̱a̱rä ɛn wa̱nɛ.I study here.
Gua̱a̱th cuŋä bäthä ja ni̱kä?Where is the bus stop?
Jɛn ɛ thiẹkä.It is nearby.
